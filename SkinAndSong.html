Iconic Songs

Five legendary tracks that shaped the Project DIVA series — and Vocaloid music as a whole.

1. World is Mine

By ryo (supercell) — One of the most streamed Vocaloid songs of all time. Miku channels a lovable, self-proclaimed queen demanding affection. Its bouncy synth-pop arrangement and relatable lyrics define an era.

2. Tell Your World

By livetune — Originally a Google Chrome advertisement, this sweeping synth-pop anthem grew into one of Miku's most emotionally resonant songs, celebrating creativity and global connection.

3. Senbonzakura (千本桜)

By Kurousa-P — A relentless fusion of shamisen motifs and driving EDM that evokes Meiji-era Japan. One of the most technically demanding charts in the series and a staple of Miku's live concerts worldwide.

4. The Disappearance of Hatsune Miku

By cosMo@Bousou-P — A technically brutal track infamous for its extreme BPM and near-impossible-to-follow lyrics. A narrative poem about Miku's identity and obsolescence — a landmark in Vocaloid storytelling.

5. Melt (メルト)

By ryo (supercell) — The tender ballad that launched Vocaloid into the mainstream. Miku narrates the nervous flutter of a first crush with warm synth pads and a melody so memorable it remains ryo's most beloved work.
