Iconic Songs

Five legendary tracks that defined the Project DIVA experience:

1. World is Mine

By ryo (supercell) — One of the most streamed Vocaloid songs of all time. Miku channels a lovable, self-proclaimed queen demanding affection. Its bouncy synth-pop arrangement and relatable lyrics turned it into the anthem of an entire generation of Vocaloid fans.

2. Tell Your World

By livetune — Originally produced for a Google Chrome advertisement, this uplifting anthem grew into one of the most emotionally resonant Miku songs. Its sweeping synth lines and optimistic message about creativity and connection remain timeless.

3. Senbonzakura (千本桜)

By Kurousa-P — A relentless fusion of traditional Japanese shamisen motifs with driving electronic beats. The song's rapid-fire pacing and Meiji-era imagery made it a staple of both the game and live Miku concerts worldwide.

4. The Disappearance of Hatsune Miku

By cosMo@Bousou-P — A technically brutal track infamous for its extreme BPM and near-impossible-to-follow lyrics. More than a song, it's a narrative poem about Miku's identity and obsolescence — a landmark in Vocaloid storytelling.

5. Melt (メルト)

By ryo (supercell) — The tender ballad that launched Vocaloid into the mainstream. Miku narrates the nervous, fluttering feeling of a first crush with warm synth pads and a melody so memorable it remains ryo's most emotionally powerful work.
