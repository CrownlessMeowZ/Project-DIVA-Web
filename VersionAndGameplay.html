Game Versions

From a humble PSP debut to a Nintendo Switch global release — 15 years of rhythm game evolution.

  1. 2009

    Hatsune Miku: Project DIVA

    The original PSP release that ignited a global phenomenon. Featuring 38 songs and a pioneering note-tap system, it introduced a generation of players to Miku and the joy of Vocaloid rhythm games.

  2. 2010

    Project DIVA 2nd

    The PSP sequel expanded the setlist, introduced all six Crypton Vocaloids as playable characters, and refined the note chart design — establishing the core formula that subsequent entries would build on.

  3. 2012

    Project DIVA F

    A landmark leap to PS Vita and PS3. The debut of the "Diva Room" social mode let players interact with characters between songs. Sharp HD visuals and touchscreen scratch notes elevated the series to a new tier.

  4. 2016

    Project DIVA X

    Available on PS Vita and PS4, X introduced a story-driven "Live Quest" campaign. Players collected voltage by performing well and unlocking themed cloud worlds — a structural experiment that deepened engagement beyond pure score-chasing.

  5. 2020

    Project DIVA MegaMix / MegaMix+

    The Nintendo Switch debut brought a dual-mode experience: the touch-heavy Arcade Mix mode alongside the classic button-press mode. MegaMix+ later brought the full 238-song catalog to PC via Steam, cementing Miku's presence on every major platform.

Gameplay Showcase

Watch how three iconic songs play out in-game across different difficulty levels:

World is Mine – Gameplay

One of the most beginner-friendly charts in the series. The steady 4/4 rhythm makes it ideal for learning note timing, yet its charming video and feel-good energy make it endlessly replayable at any skill level.

Senbonzakura – Gameplay

A mid-to-high difficulty chart that demands consistent double-tap sequences matching the shamisen arpeggios. Swipe gestures on the chorus add a layer of precision that separates good players from great ones.

The Intense Voice of Hatsune Miku – Gameplay

One of the most technically demanding charts in the entire catalog. Rapid simultaneous multi-button holds, dense sixteenth-note streams, and sudden BPM variations make Perfect completion a genuine achievement to be proud of.
