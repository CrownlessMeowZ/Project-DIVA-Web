Versions & Gameplay

From a humble PSP debut to a Nintendo Switch global release — 15 years of rhythm game evolution across five major titles.

Version Timeline

  1. 2009

    Hatsune Miku: Project DIVA (PSP)

    The PSP release that started it all. 38 songs, a pioneering note-tap system, and the first time players could dress Miku in custom modules. It launched a movement and proved Vocaloid music had a dedicated gaming audience.

  2. 2010

    Project DIVA 2nd (PSP)

    The PSP sequel introduced all six Crypton Vocaloids as playable characters for the first time, expanded the setlist significantly, and refined the chart design philosophy that subsequent entries would build on.

  3. 2012

    Project DIVA F (PS Vita / PS3)

    A landmark leap to home console and handheld HD hardware. The debut of the beloved "Diva Room" social mode and touchscreen scratch notes elevated the series. Sharp HD visuals made music videos genuinely cinematic.

  4. 2016

    Project DIVA X (PS Vita / PS4)

    Introduced a story-driven "Live Quest" campaign across five themed cloud worlds. Players collected voltage by performing well to unlock new songs and modules — a structural experiment that deepened the series beyond pure score-chasing.

  5. 2020

    Project DIVA MegaMix / MegaMix+ (Switch / PC)

    The Nintendo Switch debut brought dual-mode gameplay: MegaMix touch mode alongside the classic button-press mode. MegaMix+ later brought the full 238-song arcade catalog to PC via Steam — Miku's first true home on every major platform.

Gameplay Showcase

Three iconic songs — three levels of challenge. See how the chart design evolves from beginner-friendly to brutally expert.

World is Mine — Gameplay

A welcoming chart with steady 4/4 rhythm and well-telegraphed note patterns. The ideal entry point for new players — accessible enough to learn the basics, satisfying enough to replay indefinitely.

Senbonzakura — Gameplay

A mid-to-high difficulty chart demanding consistent double-tap sequences matching the shamisen arpeggios. Swipe gestures on the chorus separate good players from great ones — muscle memory is essential.

The Intense Voice — Gameplay

One of the most technically demanding charts in the entire catalog. Rapid multi-button holds, dense sixteenth-note streams, and sudden BPM variations make a Perfect run an achievement worth celebrating.
