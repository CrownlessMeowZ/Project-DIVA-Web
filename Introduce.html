About Project DIVA

Project DIVA is a critically acclaimed rhythm game series co-developed by SEGA and Crypton Future Media. Players tap, hold, and swipe notes in perfect sync with iconic Vocaloid tracks, unlocking hundreds of costumes and experiencing richly animated music videos. From its 2009 PSP debut to the 2022 PC release of MegaMix+, Project DIVA has defined a generation of music games.

Vocaloid Characters

Hover over a card to learn more — or visit the Characters page for full profiles.

Hatsune Miku

CV01 — The world's most iconic virtual singer. Miku's teal twin-tails and optimistic soprano voice have defined Vocaloid music since her 2007 debut.

Kagamine Rin

CV02 — Energetic and fierce, Rin's bright soprano cuts through any genre. From ballads to metal, her versatility has made her a fan favourite since 2007.

Kagamine Len

CV02 — Rin's twin brother with a warmer, lighter tenor. Len's songs span dramatic epics and comedic storytelling, always delivered with unmistakable flair.

Megurine Luka

CV03 — Bilingual, mature, and effortlessly cool. Luka's deep mezzo-soprano is the voice of sophisticated Vocaloid music, equally at home in jazz and EDM.

KAITO

The first Japanese male Vocaloid — KAITO's warm, resonant baritone and signature blue scarf have made him an enduring symbol of the entire Vocaloid movement since 2006.

MEIKO

The first Japanese female Vocaloid — MEIKO's bold, expressive alto voice and red design set the foundation for all of Crypton's future creations, debuting in 2004.
